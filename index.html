Why Taipei?

Taiwan's economic, political, and cultural center.

Taipei City is a modern cosmopolitan metropolis with a lively and diversified face, filled with exuburence. The cultural kaleidoscope of Taiwan's capital city pulses wherever you go. Incense-vailed temples dating back to dynasitc times blend seamlessly with a neon street life of a decidedly more modern era. Taipei has dozens of world-class restaurants where gourmets can sample the best regional Chinese cuisine; and for the gourmand, there are plenty of night markets serving up scrumptious evening snakcs in an environment of chaotic fun and excitement.

The polarities of Taipei are vividly present as well in the joining of the urban and natural. Just a few minutes from the heart of the city you can soak away the cares of the world in mineral-rich hot springs nestled in lush mountain foothills surrounding the Taipei Basin. And throughout the city there are plenty of trails, parks, and other oases of tranquility to lift and invigorate your spirits.

Night markets

My favorite nightmarkets in Taipei

Shilin Night Market

Shilin Night Market is a night market in Shilin District Taipei, often considered to be the largest and most famous night market in Taiwan.

Shilin Market Must Try
  • Zhong Cheng Hao Oyster Omlette
  • Hai You Pork Ribs
  • A Hui Vermicelli Noodles
Learn More

Rao He Night Market

One of the oldest night markets in Taipei, it stretches about 600 meters along Rao He Street in Songshan District and is filled with yummy food and interesting carnival games. It is located near Ciyou Temple, which was built by the local residents during the Qing Dynasty.

Rao He Market Must Try
  • Pepper Bun
  • Shi Laoban Stinky Tofu
  • Dong Fa Glutinous Rice and Mee Suan
Learn More

MiaoKou(Keelung) Night Market

The name "Miaokou" means "temple entrance" in Mandarin Chinese, as the market is located near the entrance of the Dianji Temple.

Keelung Market Must Try
  • Guabao
  • Keelung style tempura
  • Thick crab soup
